AUG 2025 · Course: Recommendation Systems

Building a recommendation system to recommend the best Amazon products to users

Recommends top Amazon products using ranking, similarity, and matrix factorization methods on user–item ratings.

Rank-based Recommendation SystemsSimilarity-based Recommendation SystemsMatrix Factorization

Model Journey & Outcome

  1. Baseline: Top‑N popularity to set a control for uplift.
  2. Phase 1: Item‑based similarity (cosine on user–item matrix) to personalize by co‑occurrence.
  3. Phase 2: User‑based k‑NN to compare taste profiles and expand coverage.
  4. Phase 3: Matrix factorization (SVD) to capture latent tastes; tuned factors & regularization.
  5. Final recommendation: SVD top‑N with fallback to item‑sim for cold items; served as a ranked list with product links.

AUG 2025 · Course: Supervised Machine Learning

Prediction system for potential customers

Classifies leads most likely to convert using logistic regression and tree-based models; includes feature engineering and evaluation.

Logistic RegressionRandom ForestsModel EvaluationFeature Engineering

Model Journey & Outcome

  1. Baseline: Majority‑class & simple EDA rules to quantify uplift.
  2. Phase 1: Pruned Decision Tree — reduced overfitting and improved interpretability.
  3. Phase 2: Tuned Random Forest — grid search over `n_estimators`, depth, and split/leaf sizes.
  4. Final recommendation: Deploy the Random Forest as the primary conversion scorer (e.g., `n_estimators=200`, `min_samples_split=10`, `min_samples_leaf=4`). Score leads daily, send the top decile to Sales, set a threshold to meet precision/recall targets, keep a nurture track for lower‑propensity segments, and emphasize referrals, profile completion, and direct contact signals; retrain and monitor Precision/Recall + F1 on fresh data.

AUG 2025 · Course: Unsupervised Learning

Customer personality segmentation

Segments customers with clustering to identify actionable personas and tailor marketing strategies.

K-Means ClusteringEDADimensionality Reduction

Model Journey & Outcome

  1. Prep: Cleaned & scaled features; winsorized outliers; explored structure with PCA scatter plots.
  2. Modeling: Compared k values via Elbow and Silhouette; selected k=4 for stable, interpretable clusters.
  3. Interpretation: Profiled centroids on spend, recency, web activity to define clear personas.
  4. Final recommendation: Adopt a 4‑segment persona map (e.g., Value Seekers, Loyal Upsellers, High‑Value Minimalists, At‑Risk Bargain Hunters). Personalize offers and channels by persona; track movement between segments monthly and refresh clusters quarterly.
